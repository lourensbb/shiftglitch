YOU'VE BEEN STUDYING WRONG YOUR ENTIRE LIFE.

Every year, millions of students sit down to study and do exactly the same thing: open their notes, read them through, close them again. Highlight in fluorescent colours. Reread the same pages the night before an exam.

It does not work. The research on this is not ambiguous.

> Hermann Ebbinghaus, 1885: humans forget roughly 70% of new information within 24 hours without active reinforcement. 140 years of cognitive science has refined that finding — but never overturned it.

70%
FORGOTTEN IN 24 HOURS

Without active reinforcement, most new memories are gone within a day. This is the Forgetting Curve — and rereading does nothing to stop it.

50%
MORE RETAINED WITH TESTING

Roediger & Karpicke (2006): students who tested themselves retained 50% more after one week than those who reread. Testing is not assessment. It is the most powerful learning method known.

10×
MORE EFFECTIVE WITH SPACING

Distributed practice over days and weeks produces dramatically stronger retention than cramming. The timing of study matters as much as the amount.
